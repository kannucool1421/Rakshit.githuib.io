Orange rice cake

  • Preparation time: less than 30 min
  • Cooking time: 30 mins to 1 hr
  • Serves: 8-10 people
Ingredients Quantity
  • Milk
    		•  1.7 litre
  • vanilla pod
    		•  1
  • Lemon, zest only,in large pieces
    		•  1
  • sugar
    		•  200g
  • Arborio rice
    		•  300g
  • Large free-range eggs, separated
    		•  5
  • Orange liquer
    		•  50mL
    Raisins 40g
  • Orange,zest only,grated, plus extra for serving
    		•  1

    Step 1:Preheat the oven to 180C/350F/Gas 4. Grease and line a 24cm/10in loose-bottomed cake tin with greaseproof paper.

    Step 2:Place the milk, vanilla pod, lemon zest and sugar in a large saucepan and bring to the boil. Add the rice and simmer on a medium to low heat for about 20–25 minutes, until the rice is al dente and has absorbed the milk but still has a creamy consistency. Remove from the heat and allow to cool. Remove vanilla pod and lemon zest.

    Step 3:In a bowl, whisk the egg yolks and liqueur until creamy. In another bowl, whisk the egg whites until stiff peaks form when the whisk is removed.

    Step 4:Add the egg yolk mixture to the cooled rice, then fold in the stiffened egg whites, followed by the raisins and orange zest. Pour in the prepared tin and bake in the oven for one hour. Serve warm or cold, sprinkled with a little extra orange zest to finish.

    Source: BBC Food

    Blackberry and apple crumble smoothie

    • Preparation time: less than 30 mins
    • Cooking time: no cooking reauired
    • Serves: 2 people
    Ingredients Quantity
  • Frozen mixed berries
    		•  300g
  • Apple cored and sliced
    		•  1
  • Semi-skimmed milk
    		•  200mL
  • Fat-free natural yogurt
    		•  150g
  • Porridge oats, with extra fibre
    		•  25g
  • Flaked almonds
    		•  10g

    Method

    Step1: Place the berries, apple, milk, yoghurt, oats and nuts in a blender and blend until smooth.

    Step2:Add 75-100ml/2½-3½floz cold water, a little at a time, to achieve a smooth consistency and blend again.

    Step3:Pour into glasses and serve.

    Source:BBC Food
